Overview

The translated sections render as real server-side content (not browser-injected), so a page-by-page comparison was possible. Overall, page-body translations are largely complete and accurate — especially on the data-heavy pages — but consistency breaks down in three systematic areas, and the two failure patterns differ sharply between the two languages.

1. Two of the six pages have no published translation at all. Managing Diabetes and Healthy Habits are unreachable in both Spanish and Chinese — their navigation links are empty and the pages are not indexed. Managing Diabetes is the most clinically important page in the set (it carries emergency low/high blood-sugar guidance, sick-day rules, and "when to call your provider" instructions), making its absence the highest-priority gap.

2. The Chinese pages share a recurring template failure. On every Chinese page the entire footer is missing, the top navigation is left untranslated in English, and the page mislabels its own language as "English." The Spanish pages keep their footer and translate their navigation.

3. Spanish and Chinese drop or distort different things. Spanish loses some body content and has a brand-name mistranslation (JDRF → "Juvenile Disaster Research Foundation"); Chinese keeps the body but loses structural elements. A few broken links and a leaked HTML tag in the page title appear in both.

Missing translated pages — two pages have no Spanish or Chinese version

On both translated sites, the navigation links for these pages point to an empty URL (<>), and neither page is indexed or reachable. They appear never to have been published in translation.

  • Managing Diabetes (/diabetes/managing-diabetes) — highest priority. The longest, most safety-critical page: hypoglycemia/hyperglycemia thresholds, warning signs, sick-day rules, and when to contact a provider. Spanish- and Chinese-speaking members have no translated access to any of it.
  • Healthy Habits (/diabetes/healthy-habits) — nutrition (the "healthy plate"), physical-activity recommendations, and exercise safety tips. No translated version exists.
Site-Wide / Recurring Issues

Recurring on all Chinese (/zh/) pages

applies to every page audited
CriticalZH The entire footer is missing on every Chinese page. About, News, Resources, "Follow Us," the URAC accreditation badges, the copyright line, and — notably — the Nondiscrimination Notice, Privacy & Legal, and "Information in Other Languages" links are all absent. Each page simply ends after its last content section. Spanish pages retain the full footer.
ModerateZH The top navigation menu is not translated. All six menu items remain in English ("Understanding Diabetes," "Prevention & Screening," etc.) on every Chinese page. The Spanish nav is translated, so this is inconsistent between the two languages.
ModerateZH The page declares its language as English. The meta-language value is English on every Chinese page (it is correctly Spanish on the Spanish pages). This affects SEO, screen readers, and "translate this page" behavior.
MinorZH Language selector reads "中国人" (a nationality, "Chinese person"). It should name the language, e.g. 中文 or 繁體中文. A Google-translation artifact rather than a hand-set label.

Recurring on Spanish (/es/) pages

applies to multiple pages
ModerateES Footer link "En la comunidad" (AZ Blue in Action) is empty. The link points to <> on every Spanish page that has a footer. The English equivalent links correctly to /about-us/az-blue-in-action.
MinorES The "X" (Twitter) social icon's alt text is translated to "incógnita." Google translated the single letter "X" to its Spanish dictionary sense ("unknown / incógnita"). Cosmetic, but visible to screen-reader users.

Affects both languages

deeper pages (with "| BCBS AZ" title)
ModerateBoth Raw HTML leaks into the page <title>. The browser-tab title renders a literal <span translate="no">BCBS AZ</span> instead of just "BCBS AZ" — e.g. Prevention & Screening, Education & Resources, and By the Numbers in both ES and ZH. An escaping bug in how the "do not translate" wrapper is emitted.
ModerateBoth "Managing Diabetes" and "Healthy Habits" nav links are empty in both languages. Both translated navigations point these two items to <> — the on-site symptom of the two missing pages.

2 · Prevention & Screening

/diabetes/prevention-and-screening
ES & ZH present
ModerateES "Screening location" mistranslated as "lugar de proyección." "Proyección" is the cinema/forecast sense of "projection," not medical screening (which would be detección / pruebas). Appears in both the section heading and the CTA, so it repeats.
ModerateBoth The "35 or older" screening detail is lost. English: "If you are 35 or older with risk factors, your doctor may screen you earlier…" Both ES ("si tiene más de un factor de riesgo") and ZH ("如果您有超過 1 個風險因素") instead say "more than one risk factor," dropping the age threshold — a factual drift shared by both.
MinorES "Path to Wellness Screenings" became "Path to Wellness Assessment." A proper program name was partly re-worded.
OKZH All sections (risk test, symptoms, A1C/FPG/OGTT tables, ethnicities) are present and accurately translated.

5 · Education & Resources

/diabetes/education-and-resources
ES & ZH present
CriticalBoth First blog link is broken in both languages. "Study Says Diabetes Rates Could Surge…" points to a Sitecore error page (/sitecore/service/notfound.aspx?…) instead of the article, in both ES and ZH.
ModerateES "JDRF" mistranslated as "Fundación para la Investigación del Desastre Juvenil." That reads "Juvenile Disaster Research Foundation" — "Diabetes" was rendered as "Desastre." The Chinese version translates it correctly (青少年糖尿病研究基金會), so this error is Spanish-only.
ModerateZH Simplified Chinese mixed into a Traditional page. A stray repeated heading renders as "教育和资源" (Simplified 资) while the rest of the page uses Traditional "教育和資源" (資). Jarring to native readers and inconsistent with the /zh/ (Traditional) intent.
MinorBoth "Understanding Diabetes Signs and Symptoms" blog link drops the locale. The last blog link points to the English URL (no /es/ or /zh/ prefix) in both languages.
MinorBoth Repeated stray "Education and Resources" headings. The page-title text is injected several times where the English had image captions/section breaks — a structural rendering glitch present in both.
